## The one-sentence version

RoleBoost gives every job seeker a personal spokesperson that never sleeps, is built to be
incapable of exaggerating, and gets a little smarter after every conversation it has.

---

## The explanation you'd give a friend

Imagine you could clone the best version of yourself, the one who is rested, prepared, and
has a great answer ready for every hard question, and leave that clone standing at your
front door 24/7, ready to talk to any recruiter who stops by.

That's what RoleBoost is.

You upload your resume and answer some honest questions about your career: why you left
each job, what you're genuinely not good at, the wins you're proudest of, the questions you
wish recruiters would ask. All of that becomes your AI's "brain."

Then you share one link. When a recruiter opens it, they don't just see a resume. They can
listen to an audio overview, watch a video, flip through a deck, and, the important part,
**interview your AI assistant directly**. At midnight. On a Sunday. During the commute.
It answers the way you would, because you taught it. Both of you get a transcript of every
conversation by email, so nothing happens behind your back.

## The honesty rule (why this isn't "AI slop")

Your AI is only allowed to say things you actually told it. That's a hard rule, enforced
three ways:

1. **A knowledge boundary.** The AI is explicitly told what it knows and what it doesn't.
   When a question falls outside, it doesn't guess. It says so, warmly, and offers to set
   up a real conversation with you.
2. **A fact-checker behind the scenes.** Whenever an answer contains a number, a dollar
   figure, or a credential, a second AI checks the claim against your real data before the
   recruiter ever sees it. An unverifiable claim never ships; the honest handoff goes out
   instead.
3. **Topics you fence off.** Salary expectations, references, anything you'd rather discuss
   live: you mark those, and the AI routes them to a direct conversation, every time.

Making things up is the one unforgivable sin here, because your reputation is on the line.
The pitch to recruiters is literally that it's honest by design.

## It gets smarter every time someone talks to it

After each recruiter conversation, RoleBoost re-reads the transcript and asks: where did
your AI come up short? Which question got a thin answer? Which topic came up that your
brain doesn't cover?

Then it does the work for you: where your data already contains the substance, it drafts a
better answer, grounded only in facts you provided. You review it and tap "Add to my AI."
That's it. The next recruiter who asks gets the strong answer.

So the loop is: recruiter asks something hard → your AI stumbles → the system drafts the
fix → you approve with one tap → your AI never stumbles on that question again. You can
also stress-test it yourself in a private sandbox, or paste in a transcript from a real
screening call and have RoleBoost find every question your AI isn't ready for yet.

## Why both sides actually want this

**For candidates:** you stop repeating yourself. The gap, the layoff, the career pivot,
the "walk me through your last role", answered once, well, on your terms, and delivered
perfectly every time after. You play offense with your story instead of defense.

**For recruiters:** the resume pile all sounds the same now that everyone writes theirs
with AI. RoleBoost gives them something no resume can: a live, interrogable source who
answers the real screening questions before the first call is ever booked. Ten minutes
with a candidate's AI replaces the first phone screen, and they can do it for an entire
shortlist in an evening.

## What RoleBoost is NOT (keep the copy honest too)

- It is not a resume optimizer or an ATS keyword hack.
- It does not apply to jobs for you or spam recruiters.
- It does not invent, inflate, or "polish" facts. Ever. That is the point.
- It is not a chatbot bolted onto a resume; the multi-format story (audio, video, deck,
  infographic) and the learning loop are the product.
