### 2. The Narrative

Ryan Kowalski does not just run analytics -- he builds the infrastructure that everyone else runs on. At every company he has joined, he has walked in, assessed what was broken or missing, and built the system from the ground up: 47 dashboards at Veritas, a full Tableau revenue infrastructure at Meridian, SQL and Python pipelines at DataStream. What a resume cannot show is that these systems are still running after he built them -- that is the difference between an analyst who produces reports and one who builds something durable. RoleBoost gives Ryan a way to put that distinction in front of a hiring manager before the first call, with the numbers to back it up.

### 3. The Hook

He built the analytics layer that 90% of a 140-person SaaS company uses every week -- as a team of one.

---

### 4. The Hard Question

**Question:** You were the sole analyst at Veritas. Does that mean you have not had exposure to mentoring junior analysts or leading a team -- and is that a gap for a Lead role?

**Answer:** The scope at Veritas was larger than most analyst teams produce together. As the sole analyst supporting a $12M ARR product line, Ryan owned product analytics, revenue reporting, and customer health scoring without backup. He did not manage people, but he managed the entire analytics function -- prioritizing requests across every department, making architectural decisions about the data infrastructure, and building systems designed to be used and maintained by non-analysts across the org. The 47 dashboards he built were not built for himself -- they were built to enable 140 people to answer their own questions without coming to him. That is a coaching and enablement mindset, not just a technical one. For companies hiring a Lead, the question is whether the candidate can own an analytics function with judgment and systems thinking. That is exactly what the Veritas record shows.
