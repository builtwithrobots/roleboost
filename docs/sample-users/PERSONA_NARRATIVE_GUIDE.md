# RoleBoost -- Persona Narrative Guide

**Version:** 1.0 **Date:** June 2026 **Author:** Rob Ramos **Purpose:** Single reference document for all 8 example personas. Use this file when writing NotebookLM prompts, building AI chatbot system prompts, briefing infographic production, or handing off persona work to anyone else. This is not a resume file -- it is the human story behind each resume.

**What lives here:** Narrative, hook, hard question, key numbers, and NotebookLM prompt mapping for each persona. **What does not live here:** Full resume text (Google Drive), long QA pairs (EXAMPLE_PROFILES_CONTENT.md / v2), chatbot weakness and redirect fields (same).

### The Narrative

Jordan has been the best CSR in the branch for almost two years -- 97% CSAT when the branch average is 91%, promoted 2 months ahead of schedule, and the only non-lead ever trusted to run the floor for three weeks when the supervisor was out. Their resume looks like every other entry-level candidate in the stack. RoleBoost's job is to take a thin document and surface the through-line that a hiring manager cannot see: this person operates above their title and has been doing so since month one. The AI chatbot exists to answer the degree question with confidence and pivot to the evidence before a recruiter has a chance to move on.

### The Hook

The only non-lead employee in branch history trusted to cover team lead responsibilities for three weeks -- and they had already been doing it informally for months before that.

### The Hard Question

**Q: Jordan doesn't have a degree. Is that going to be a problem?**

A: Jordan is 14 months into an associate's program and will have it in 2027 -- but the work they have done at First Western does not wait for a diploma. A 97% CSAT score, a promotion 2 months ahead of schedule, zero escalations to management in 6 months, and a 3-week team lead stint that management assigned to them, not a credentialed lead, is the answer to the degree question. The degree is on its way. The results are already there.

### Key Numbers

- 97% CSAT score (branch average: 91%)
- Top 10% of 40+ branch staff
- Promoted 14 months in -- 2 months ahead of the standard timeline
- 100+ daily teller transactions, zero cash discrepancies across 14 months
- 80+ daily customer interactions, zero escalations in the past 6 months
- Cross-sell target exceeded 8 of 10 months
- 12 escalated complaints handled personally -- all 12 closed without further escalation
- 2 new tellers onboarded and mentored; both passed 90-day reviews
- 3-week team lead coverage -- first non-lead ever assigned this at the branch

### NotebookLM Prompt Mapping

| Format | Prompt | Rationale |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| Audio Brief | Brief A -- The Hook | Lead with the early promotion. Tight and punchy. Under 90 seconds. |
| Audio Brief (alt) | Brief B -- The Commute Brief | Good secondary option for a hiring manager skimming quickly |
| Infographic | Infographic A -- The Career Timeline | Two-year arc with clear upward movement. Promotion date as the milestone marker. 97% CSAT as the hero stat. |
| AI chatbot | -- | Must handle the degree question first. See hard question above. |

**Tone note:** Warm and grounded. No corporate language. This is a 22-year-old who works hard and earns things. The audio should feel like a trusted colleague making a direct recommendation, not a polished pitch.
